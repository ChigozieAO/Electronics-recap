- The only difference between a boltt of lightning and the
  spark between your finger and the door knob on a dry day
  is quantity.
- Resting electrons: A group of negative electrons on a
  surface causes the surfacw to be negatively charged
  (Negative Static Electric Charge)
- Moving electrons: A stream of moving electrons is called
  an Electrical circuit
- Missing electrons: Mechanical friction, light, heat or a
  chemical reaction may remove electrons from a surface
  (Positive Static electrical charge)
- Static charges are caused by mechanical friction
- An immobile static charge flows through a conductor as an
  electrical current, it then resumes its static state on
  the electroscope foil leaves.
- Electrical current flows from a region of high charge or
  potential to a region of low potential
- If an electrical current flows in only one direction,
  whether steadily or in pulses, its called Direct Current
- Current (I): Current is the quantity of electrons passing
  a given point. the unit of current is the
  AMPHERE(6.28\*10^18)
- Voltage (V or E): Voltage is electrical pressure or force.
  voltage is sometimes referred to as potential, if we
  compare current to water flowing through a pipe, then
  voltage is the water pressure
- Power (P): the work performed by an electrical current is
  called power. the unit of power is the WATT. the power of
  a direct current is its voltage times its current.
- Resistance (R): the resistance of a conductor is ts
  voltage drop divided by the current flowing through the
  conductor.
